Below is a brief listing of the various leadership roles of the SPC that are offered in K-grams. They should give you an overview of the positions and expectations of the people in their positions. Don't forget that there are also many other levels of volunteering in K-grams, from Swing Kids to pen pals and in-between. This is simply the overview of it all!


Executive Director

The Executive Director serves as the chief officer for all of K-grams and its programming. S/he represents the organization externally to all communities and generates initiatives and support for all internal issues. The Executive Director oversees the entire programming processes while setting the agenda and scope of weekly functions, as well as long-range objectives. S/he works on pulling together all parts of the program in order to promote the mission and goals of K-grams.

Associate Director

The Associate Director coordinates all internal aspects of K-grams and the entire programming team. S/he works directly with the Executive Director and other Directors to coordinate all major events and ensure the overall operation of the program. S/he is instrumental in seeking personnel to fill roles and is also a resource for many to turn to with questions and for support. Together, the Associate and Executive Directors make up the Executive Team, which is instrumental in making all major decisions and ensuring the day-to-day success of K-grams.

Finance Committee

The Finance Committee works directly with the Associate Director to assist with the financial operations of K-grams. Their responsibilities include attending residence hall council meetings to request funding, handling and distributing T-shirts and fundraiser raffle tickets, and organizing other internal finance operations.

Marketing Committee

The Marketing Committee works directly with the Executive Team to assist with a variety of functions including regularly updating the website, creating publications, and maintaining the K-grams historic records. Responsibilities of this committee involve a wide range of knowledge and technical skills. Some tasks include taking pictures at all events, gathering memorabilia from projects, events, and meetings, and recording the spirit that embodies K-grams. In addition, this committee will maintain the website with regular updates. It will also help generate and distribute the bi-monthly newsletters to pen pals and parents, as well as offer ideas and suggestions for other specific publications throughout the year. The Marketing Committee ensures that everyone involved in K-grams (both internal and external audiences) receives current information in a timely manner, while also preserving the lasting memories that are made each day.


Pen Pal Directors  

The Pen Pal Directors oversee all interactions between the college and elementary school pen pals and ensure that the pen pal program is running smoothly and efficiently. Working closely with the Hall Heads, they assure that all letters are collected and delivered to the schools and help to plan edu-active (educational and interactive) classroom projects and other elementary events throughout the year, such as college day, and campus visits. In addition, the Pen Pal Directors are responsible for maintaining the main internal database and updating pen pal changes as needed throughout the year. This is possible based on the information they receive from the Hall Heads at each week's meeting. Working with Hall Heads, Swing Kids, and other Directors, the Pen Pal Directors work to create positive relationships between and among college and elementary pen pals and ensure the continued, overall success of the pen pal letter exchange and project planning.

Hall Heads

Each Hall Head has a partner with whom they work directly throughout the entire year. Each pair of Hall Heads ensures timely collection and delivery of all pen pal letters each month, as well as creates and executes edu-active (educational and interactive) projects in each classroom. They work to build community between pen pals, Swing Kids, and teachers, and also help pen pals to become further involved in K-grams. In addition, they help increase the college pen pal's connection with her/his elementary pen pal, and with the program as a whole. Hall Heads are the link between the elementary schools, college pen pals, and the SPC.

Swing Kids

Swing Kids supervise the letter collection each month and encourage college pen pal involvement for one elementary school classroom. Swing Kids work together with their Hall Heads and the other Swing Kids to develop a sense of pride and community within the hall. The Swing Kids are an integral part of K-grams, as they serve as the direct link between the college pen pals and the rest of the program. Swing Kids are the pen pals' source of information about letters, projects, and other kids programs.

Elementary School Director

The Elementary School Director serves as the main liaison between K-grams and the elementary schools. S/he sets up all preliminary conversations with principals and/or teachers at the beginning and end of each year, and remains the resource for any questions in regards to school policy and information. This role requires a very personable and patient person who is able to communicate clearly and effectively in order to ensure the success of letter exchanges, BookMARK, and Kids-Fair. Overall, this Director works with the Executive team to help better facilitate communication and understanding between the college and elementary communities.

BookMARK Director

The BookMARK (Mentoring And Reading with Kids) Director oversees the BookMARK program. S/he is in charge of organizing Coordinators and volunteers for each session, coordinating transportation, gathering feedback, responding to questions and concerns of others, and working with the Elementary Director to set up session dates and times with teachers. This role entails a great deal of interaction with other people through email, phone, and meetings, plus the coordination of many volunteers.

BookMARK Coordinator

BookMARK Coordinators supervise and organize a weekly BookMARK session. Their role includes contacting and coordinating all volunteers for the weekly session, communicating with their BookMARK teacher to ensure success of their session, and meeting with the BookMARK Director and other Coordinators for weekly updates. Through constant communication and initiative the BookMARK Coordinators ensure the daily success of each individual session, while at the same time working to strengthen the BookMARK program as a whole.

Campus Relations Director

The Campus Relations Director oversees recruitment of college students and groups to participate in K-grams, as well as the interactions between K-grams and the University community at large. This Director works closely with the Operations Director, Campus Liaisons, and Executive Team to ensure that the campus is familiar with K-grams. The Campus Relations Director serves as a leader and main resource for the Campus Liaisons, answering any questions about K-grams and keeping them excited and enthused throughout the year. S/he also supervises the involvement of student groups in K-grams, specifically those participating in Kids-Fair. This Director should be familiar with the all aspects of K-grams and be able to bring all parts together when needed.

Campus Liaisons

Campus Liaisons work primarily to encourage, recruit, and inform pen pals, BookMARKers, and student groups to be involved with K-grams. These responsibilities are highlighted at different times during the year, specifically with their involvement in recruiting pen pals during Welcome Week and recruiting student groups and Buddies to participate in Kids-Fair. Liaisons also work to increase the involvement of student group members in other K-grams projects and BookMARK sessions throughout the year. Liaisons work with the Campus Relations Director in order to spread the word about K-grams on campus, encourage students and organizations campus-wide to become involved with K-grams, and develop edu-active booth ideas for Kids-Fair.

Operations Director

The Operations Director works with the internal structure of K-grams in order to keep such a large and diverse organization successful. Communicating and working closely with the other Directors and the Operations Committee, s/he oversees administrative and functional aspects of K-grams. Such tasks include developing campus-wide publicity strategies, reserving equipment for meetings and events, preparing publicity and marketing materials, supervising specific logistics for events such as Festigrams, K-Day, Kids-Fair, socials, and the year-end banquet. This Director understands how K-grams runs on a daily basis and works to improve and streamline these tasks.

Operations Committee

The Operations Committee works directly with the Operations Director to assist in the administrative functions of K-grams. Included in this role is the responsibility of publicizing K-grams and specific events taking place throughout the year. In addition, this committee will help prepare marketing materials (e.g. Diag boards, banners, signs, etc.), as well as help handle logistics to ensure events and activities run smoothly. Such tasks may include reserving equipment, requesting donations from businesses, and recruiting other SPC members to help with the events. This committee works closely as a team and helps other Directors to improve the overall K-grams experience for all U-M students.
