I'm a Hall Head!
So what do I do now?

As a Hall Head, your role is a pivotal one that is interactive with the elementary schools, the college pen pals, and the SPC. In most instances, this role requires self-initiative to enhance the letter writing process and exchanges, as well as to create and execute projects as much as you desire! You will work directly with a Hall Head partner assigned to your residence hall to ensure that letters are exchanged accurately and timely each month, that each classroom does one "edu-active" (educational and interactive) project per semester, and that pen pals stay involved with the program in other ways
Main Responsibilities:
  • Work closely with your Hall Head partner to actively communicate with each teacher at your school regarding all aspects of the program. You are the link between the University and the elementary school!
  • Ensure timely letter collection and delivery through close contact with your Swing Kids.
  • Strengthen personal relations among Swing Teams and pen pals within your residence hall.
  • Keep Swing Kids, pen pals, and teachers informed of all upcoming events and other pertinent information.
  • Attend weekly meetings with the Pen Pal Directors and the other Hall Heads; update them on the status of projects and other exchange ideas.
  • Maintain regular communication with the BookMARK Coordinator(s) who are working with a classroom in your school.
  • Serve as a liaison to your hall council, informing them of K-grams activities and encouraging involvement.
  • Communicate project ideas to each teacher to create a project beneficial to each individual classroom and all pen pals.
  • Assist Swing Kids in encouraging pen pals to generate project ideas; help spur active participation and attendance with projects.
A little more concrete...
  • Every other Friday, between you and your Hall Head partner, someone MUST get to your school! Letters will either have to be delivered and/or picked up! The teachers will be expecting you!
  • Each time you go to your school, each teacher should (MUST!!) hand you a completed "Teacher Update" form. Turn in the form--even if it is BLANK--at your weekly meeting to one of the Directors. THIS IS ABSOLUTELY NECESSARY!
  • The Teacher Update form includes a very important section where teachers will write any changes in their class lists, such as a student who moved away. It is imperative that you turn in a Teacher Update form at your weekly meetings after each letter drop-off or pick-up in order for the changes to be processed.
  • Once the change is processed, the affected Swing Kid(s) will be contacted by email; at the next weekly meeting, you will receive a processed change sheet and updated class list for your own records. Be prepared--Swing Kids will come to YOU with questions about this!
  • Work with your Swing Kids to build pen pal relations. Maybe sponsor a letter-writing party, organize a pizza party, go rock-n-bowling...it is all up to you.
  • Assemble supplies, personnel, and transportation for each project.
  • Submit Project Information Forms for each project.
