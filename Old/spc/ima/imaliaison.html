I'm a Campus Liaison!
So what do I do now?

As a Liaison, you will be responsible for spreading the word about K-grams on campus and recruiting college students and groups to participate in K-grams throughout the year. These tasks will be highlighted at key times during the year, specifically when recruiting pen pals and BookMARKers in the fall and developing "edu-active" (educational and interactive) booth ideas with student groups for Kids-Fair. One primary responsibility will be act as a contact for student groups involved in K-grams, while maintaining positive relationships with them.  You are the main external K-grams representative on campus, so it is very important that you are friendly and dependable with the University community at large!

Main Responsibilities:

  • Keep weekly contact with the Campus Relations Director.
  • Attend all Liaison and monthly SPC meetings.
  • Recruit pen pals to participate in K-grams during Welcome Week.
  • Generate overall enthusiasm and excitement about K-grams across the campus.
  • Spread the word about BookMARK to all areas of campus; recruit volunteers to participate.
  • Recruit and sign-up new student groups to be involved in K-grams.
  • Make presentations about getting involved at student group meetings.
  • Develop creative Kids-Fair booth ideas with student groups.
  • Inform student group contacts of all necessary steps for Kids-Fair.
  • Recruit college students and pen pals to sign-up as Kids-Fair Buddies.

Ongoing Notes:

  • Promote student involvement in all areas of K-grams (BookMARK, Kids-Fair, projects, campus visits).
  • Develop good working relationships with pen pals, BookMARKers, and student group contacts for future involvement.
  • Strategize recruitment techniques and other ways to get students involved in K-grams
  • Ensure student groups are fully prepared before sponsoring a booth at Kids-Fair
  • Contact the Campus Relations Director with any concerns or questions.
