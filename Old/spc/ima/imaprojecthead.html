I'm a Project Head!
So what do I do now?

As a Project Head, you will create and execute projects. Each classroom must participate in at least one project per semester. Using the Swing Kids as a resource, the projects should be fun, educational, and allow for meaningful interaction between pen pals. You will also work with your hall's Letter Head in maintaining contact with the teachers and in soliciting project ideas for your school.
Main responsibilities:
  • Work closely with your hall's Letter Head to actively communicate with each teacher at your school regarding all aspects of the program. You are the link between the University and the elementary school!
  • Communicate project ideas to each teacher to create a project beneficial to each individual classroom and all pen pals.
  • Assist Swing Kids in encouraging pen pals to generate project ideas; Help spur active participation and attendance with projects.
  • Attend weekly meetings to update Pen Pal Directors and other Heads on status of projects in each classroom and exchange ideas.
A little more concrete...
  • Every other Friday, either you or your hall's Letter Head, MUST get to your school! Letters will either have to be delivered and/or picked up, and teachers will be expecting you!
  • Each time you go to your school, each teacher MUST hand you a completed "Teacher Update" form. Turn in the form--even if it is BLANK--at your weekly meeting. THIS IS ABSOLUTELY NECESSARY!
  • The Teacher Update form includes a very important section where teachers will write any changes in their class lists, such as a student who moved away. It is imperative that you (or the Letter Head) turn in a Teacher Update form after each letter drop-off or pick-up, in order for the changes to be processed.
  • Assemble supplies, personnel, and transportation for each project.
  • Submit Project Information Forms for each project.
  • Help your Letter Head with letter collection and overall communication with your pen pals
