Holmes - Mrs. Stewart 
Session Coordinator: Katie Lorenz (ktlorenz@umich.edu)

The moment the volunteers walk through the door of Mrs. Stewart’s 1st grade classroom, the faces of the students quickly brighten as they greet us with huge smiles! The teacher splits the kids into groups and with the help of a volunteer, each group picks the perfect spot to go sit. We read stories that the students had picked out from their school library moments earlier and then play fun math card games. When it’s time for us to leave, we collect our heartfelt hugs and leave the classroom with the satisfaction of knowing that we had brightened the day of 20 first grade students. :)

Holmes - Mrs. Cantrell
Session Coordinator: Andrea Miller (andreamm@umich.edu)

It’s great walking into Mrs. Cantrell’s 1st grade classroom-all the kids look up, smile and yell, “hi!!” Then we break up into groups and work on their spelling. They practice by finger painting, twisting pipe cleaners, or making letters with marshmallows and pretzels. When it’s finally time to go, all the kids line up to hug us goodbye and ask if we would come back tomorrow. We always look forward to next week!

Pittsfield - Mrs. Lynch

Carpenter - Mrs. Brower
Session Coordinator: David Peckerman (depeckerm@umich.edu)

When you walk into Mrs. Brower’s first grade classroom at Carpenter Elementary, you suddenly feel as though time has reversed itself. Almost in an instant, you have escaped the chaos of college life and are back in the world of mini-desks, story time, and simplicity. This experience is the epitome of being mutually beneficial. While the kids are excited about learning with their 'cool' college buddies, you, at least for the hour, can take a break from college and just have fun. Come join us on Thursday mornings for this unique experience!

Vetal - Mr. Davis
Session Coordinator: David Hoffman (djhoffma@umich.edu)
As we exit the highway, the excitement grows as we near Vetal and Mr. Davis' fifth grade class. We walk inside and the classroom erupts- it’s like we just won the World Series. Mr. Davis gives a short speech, asks the kids if they want to play outside, looks at the college buddies and says "David, take them outside." We have fun with football, jump rope, singing, tag, and dodge ball. As we attempt to leave, there are hugs all around and at least one college buddy is always pulled along toward the lunchroom so they can hang out with us for just a little while longer. There couldn’t be a better way to start your weekend!
